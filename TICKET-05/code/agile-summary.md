


Agile Manifesto Values

The Agile Manifesto defines four core values that guide modern software development:

1. Individuals and Interactions over Processes and Tools

Agile emphasizes the importance of people working together effectively rather than relying heavily on strict processes or tools. Communication, collaboration, and teamwork are essential for successful project delivery.

2. Working Software over Comprehensive Documentation

While documentation is useful, Agile prioritizes delivering functional software that provides real value to users. Frequent delivery of working software allows teams to receive feedback earlier and improve the product continuously.

3. Customer Collaboration over Contract Negotiation

Agile encourages continuous collaboration with customers and stakeholders throughout the development process. Instead of focusing strictly on contractual requirements, teams work closely with users to understand their needs and adjust the product accordingly.

4. Responding to Change over Following a Plan

Agile teams accept that requirements may change during development. Rather than rigidly following an initial plan, Agile allows teams to adapt quickly to new information, feedback, or market demands.

Key Scrum Principles Learned

Scrum is one of the most widely used Agile frameworks. Some key principles include:

Iterative Development

Scrum projects are divided into short cycles called sprints, usually lasting 1–4 weeks. Each sprint focuses on delivering a small but usable product increment.

Daily Communication

Scrum teams hold a daily stand-up meeting where members briefly discuss what they worked on, what they plan to work on next, and any obstacles they face.

Defined Roles

Scrum includes three main roles:

Product Owner: Defines product requirements and priorities.

Scrum Master: Facilitates the Scrum process and removes obstacles.

Development Team: Builds and delivers the product increment.

Continuous Improvement

After each sprint, teams conduct a sprint retrospective to review what worked well and what can be improved in the next sprint.
